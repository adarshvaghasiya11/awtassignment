Technology

The average business receives 10,000 alerts every day from the various software tools it uses to monitor for intruders, malware and other threats. Cybersecurity staff often find themselves inundated with data they need to sort through to manage their cyber defenses.

Protecting employees from harmful content was a far more straightforward task once upon a time. COVID-19 changed all of that in an instant. The new hybrid work model has forced IT professionals to rethink their security strategy game plans. As threats continue to emerge, it becomes an ongoing game of cat and mouse for IT teams to outmaneuver cybercriminals. One of the biggest challenges is keeping up with the latest forms of malware. Kaspersky detected 516,617 malicious installation packages in Q1 2022. Any one of these packages could be unknowingly downloaded by a remote worker.
